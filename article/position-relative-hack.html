Position Relative

Last night whilst building a slider that required the use of position absolute for the slides that were hidden. To ensure the slider all stayed in one place, this required a div to hold the slider content with the CSS property of position:relative. This stopped the hidden slides from jumping to the top left of the document.
However, when adding this CSS property to elements, it removes it from the DOM flow (a quick search of Stackoverflow shows this is a common problem). This causes the containing div to collapse and have no height or width. In a fixed width build, this would simply require the containing div to have a specified height, and be done with it.
My situation was a little more complex because its for a responsive build. The images in the slider therefore have no defined height, as this is dependent on the size of the browser.
A solution suggested to me was to give the holding element a height:0, and the padding-top a percentage. As I’m working with portrait images, I gave the holding div a padding-top of 150%. This takes the width, and makes the padding at the top of the image 150% of the width, allowing the slider to become fully responsive.
Keep an eye out for the full tutorial I’m going to post later.
