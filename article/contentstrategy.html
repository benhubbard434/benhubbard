Having a content strategy is important

And here’s why. http://www.factbrowser.com/facts/12724//content creator. This means that there is time pressure on the other 91% of people responsible for their company blog, juggling responsibilities.

  • Maintaining momentum: This is one of the hardest parts of maintaining a blog when you have other responsibilities - convincing some management teams of the value of the blog can be difficult, especially if you are split between sales and marketing. Having a content strategy, including goals measured against KPIs can help justify to senior management that you need to spend time working on it, and can show the value of a well run blog can bring to the business.

  • Good content = good SEO: Planning content to be relevant to your site can help build a successful SEO strategy. Search engines are determined to build better algorithms to rank content based on the quality of it. Clear, concise and well written content ranks well. Alongside this, it’s important that your title and URL match your content. This is part of the on page optimisation that you should be doing.

  • Keeping it fresh: Fail to prepare and prepare to fail is the well known expression. Planning your content in advance increases the quality of your content, as well as ensuring that you never double post something.

  • Integrating your marketing: Knowing when you are creating content is important. It means you can plan other areas around your content, for example promoting your new blog post via your email marketing, and driving traffic towards the latest news using social media.

You can compare a company website to a box of chocolates. A box of chocolates can be packaged with the most luxurious packaging, if the chocolates inside are poor quality, no one will buy or recommend them. A website that looks drab but has great content is far better than the inverse. Check out Wikipedia as an example!
